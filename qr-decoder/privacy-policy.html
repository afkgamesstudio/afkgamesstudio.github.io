1. Corporate Declaration and Entity Scope

This Privacy Policy governs the data practices of AFK GAMES STUDIO (“the Studio,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) regarding the mobile application QR Decoder (the “App”). The Studio operates under a strict Data Minimization Protocol. [cite_start]We do not require account registration, and we do not directly collect or store Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as legal names, physical addresses, or contact credentials[cite: 202].

2. Privacy by Design: On-Device Processing

[cite_start]

The App’s architecture is engineered to prioritize user privacy through local processing[cite: 23, 26]:

    [cite_start]
  • On-Device Execution: All scanning operations, including barcode/QR decoding, Text Recognition (OCR), and document processing, are performed strictly on your device using local machine learning models[cite: 88, 89].
    • [cite_start]
  • Zero Transmission of Media: Actual image content, camera frames, and scanned documents are never transmitted to AFK GAMES STUDIO or Google servers[cite: 89, 91].
    • [cite_start]
  • Local Data Segregation: Your scan history is stored exclusively in a local Room database on your device[cite: 23, 188]. [cite_start]We do not have access to your data, nor do we implement cloud-based synchronization or backups[cite: 24, 236].

3. Automated Third-Party Data Processing

[cite_start]

To maintain a free and reliable service, the App integrates Enterprise SDKs provided by Google[cite: 30, 86]:

    [cite_start]
  • Google AdMob: May collect persistent identifiers (such as the Android Advertising ID), approximate location derived from IP addresses, and ad interaction metrics to deliver non-sensitive or personalized advertisements[cite: 6, 9, 93, 94].
    • [cite_start]
  • Google ML Kit Telemetry: May ingest non-identifiable technical telemetry (model performance, usage frequency, and device diagnostics) to improve service reliability[cite: 33, 90]. [cite_start]It does not collect the content of your scans[cite: 38, 91].
    • [cite_start]
  • Direct Network Requests (Coil): When a user explicitly chooses to view a remote image from a scanned code, the App facilitates a direct HTTPS connection between the device and the source server[cite: 35, 240]. [cite_start]This interaction is not logged, monitored, or stored by the Studio[cite: 36, 241].
